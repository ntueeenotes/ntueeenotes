### EE1102 · Physics Foundation — Grade: B+ · 🟠 Challenging

**What's covered:** Optics (reflection, refraction, interference), electromagnetism (electric fields, Coulomb's law, Maxwell's equations), and quantum physics (photoelectric effect, wave-particle duality, atomic structure).

**Assessment:** OASIS Midterms (10% × 2) · Labs (10%) · Assignment (10%) · Finals (60%)

**Materials included**
- Final summary notes — 13 chapters, 13 pages
- Midterm summary notes — 10 pages
- Tutorial answers (Week 3–13)
- Take-home assignment answers (10%)
- Labsheet with answers (10%)
- 4 past year papers with full solutions: 2021 S2, 2021 S1, 19/20 S1, 18/19 S2

**How to tackle it:** Focus on tutorials and OASIS practice for midterms. Quantum physics chapters get hard without H2 Physics background — this module also feeds directly into EE3101 Electromagnetics in Year 3, so the investment pays off twice.

### EE2101 · Circuit Analysis — Grade: A · 🟠 Challenging

**What's covered:** Analytical techniques for real-world circuit behaviour — nodal/mesh analysis, Thevenin/Norton, AC analysis, transients.

**Assessment:** OASIS Quizzes · Assignments (10%) · Finals (60%)

**Materials included**
- Tutorial answers (Week 1–12)
- Midterm OASIS workings
- Step-by-step solutions for Assignments 1 & 2 (full marks)
- Compiled finals summary notes
- 4 past year papers with full solutions

**How to tackle it:** Difficulty ramps hard after mid-semester. The bottleneck isn't knowing the methods — it's picking the *right* method for the circuit in front of you. The compiled summary notes are built around exactly that decision tree.

### IE2106 · Engineering Mathematics 1 — Grade: A · 🟠 Challenging

**What's covered:** Probability distributions, statistical analysis (CIs, hypothesis testing), numerical methods (fixed-point iteration, interpolation, Euler/Heun/Runge-Kutta), Laplace transforms, PDEs.

**Assessment:** Midterms (15% × 2) · Assignments (5% × 2) · Finals (60%)

**Materials included**
- Tutorial answers (Week 1–12)
- OASIS practice question workings
- Step-by-step full-mark solutions for Assignments 1 & 2
- 4 past year papers with full solutions

**How to tackle it:** Midterms demand precision (8 decimal places — yes, really). The assignment solutions are templates you can adapt; the past papers cover the full range of probability distributions (normal, Poisson, binomial) you need to be fluent in.

## 💡 General Strategy — What Actually Worked

Patterns that held across most modules:

1. **Past year papers are non-negotiable.** They're the single highest-leverage prep tool. Solve them, don't just read them.
2. **Match prep to platform.** OASIS-based assessments reward question-bank grinding. Traditional paper exams reward tutorial mastery.
3. **Build your own cheatsheet** even for modules without one allowed — the act of compiling it is the revision.
4. **Start design assignments early.** Especially EE2102, EE3015, and (looking ahead) EE4523.
